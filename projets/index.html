Planets of the Universe

A planet is an astronomical object orbiting a star or stellar remnant that is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity, is not massive enough to cause thermonuclear fusion, and has cleared its neighbouring region of planetesimals.

Types of Planets

  1. Dwarf

    A dwarf planet is a planetary-mass object that is neither a planet nor a satellite.

  2. Terrestrial

    A terrestrial planet, telluric planet or rocky planet is a planet that is composed primarily of silicate rocks or metals.

  3. Gas Giant

    A gas giant is a large planet that is not primarily composed of rock or other solid matter.

Recently Discovered Planets

  • Kepler-62e

    Kepler-62e is a super-Earth exoplanet discovered in orbit around the star Kepler-62

  • Tau Ceti e

    Tau Ceti e is an unconfirmed planet that may be orbiting the nearby Sun-like star Tau Ceti.
